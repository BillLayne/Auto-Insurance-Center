Understanding Your Coverage Options

North Carolina requires specific coverage, but the right protection goes beyond minimums. Let's find what works for you.

Liability Coverage

Required by NC law. Covers damage and injuries you cause to others.

  • • Bodily Injury: $50k/$100k minimum
  • • Property Damage: $50k minimum
  • • Protects your assets from lawsuits

Comprehensive

Protection against non-collision damage to your vehicle.

  • • Theft and vandalism
  • • Weather damage (hail, floods)
  • • Animal collisions

Collision

Covers damage to your car from accidents, regardless of fault.

  • • Accident damage
  • • Single-car accidents
  • • Hit-and-run incidents

Uninsured Motorist

Required in NC. Protects when at-fault driver has no insurance.

  • • Medical expenses
  • • Lost wages
  • • Property damage

North Carolina Minimum Requirements

State Minimums (50/100/50)

Required Coverage:

  • 📌 $50,000 Bodily Injury per person
  • 📌 $100,000 Bodily Injury per accident
  • 📌 $50,000 Property Damage
  • 📌 $50,000/$100,000 Uninsured Motorist

Penalties for No Insurance:

  • ⚠️ $50-$150 fine first offense
  • ⚠️ License suspension possible
  • ⚠️ SR-22 requirement (3 years)
  • ⚠️ Higher future rates

Pro Tip: While these are minimums, most accidents exceed these limits. Consider 100/300/100 coverage to better protect your assets.

Common Auto Insurance Questions

How much auto insurance do I really need?

While NC requires 50/100/50 coverage, we recommend at least 100/300/100 to protect your assets. If you own a home or have savings, consider even higher limits plus an umbrella policy.

Should I get comprehensive and collision coverage?

If your vehicle is worth more than $4,000 or you can't afford to replace it, these coverages are worth it. For newer vehicles or if you have a loan, they're typically required.

What affects my insurance rates in North Carolina?

Your driving record, age, location, vehicle type, credit score, and coverage levels all impact rates. North Carolina also uses a "Safe Driver Incentive Plan" that can increase rates for violations.

How can I lower my premium?

Bundle policies, maintain a clean driving record, improve your credit, increase deductibles wisely, and ask about all available discounts. Shopping around annually is also key - rates vary significantly between companies.
