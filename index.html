Understanding Your Coverage Options

Choose the right protection for your vehicle and financial security

🚗

Liability Coverage

Covers damage and injuries you cause to others. Required by NC law.

  • ✓ Bodily injury: $50,000 per person
  • ✓ Total per accident: $100,000
  • ✓ Property damage: $50,000
🌪️

Comprehensive

Protection against non-collision damage to your vehicle.

  • ✓ Theft and vandalism
  • ✓ Weather damage
  • ✓ Animal collisions
💥

Collision

Covers damage to your car from accidents, regardless of fault.

  • ✓ Accident damage
  • ✓ Single-car accidents
  • ✓ Hit-and-run incidents
🛡️

Uninsured Motorist

Protects you when the at-fault driver has no insurance.

  • ✓ Medical expenses
  • ✓ Lost wages
  • ✓ Pain and suffering

North Carolina Auto Insurance Requirements

Stay legal and protected on NC roads

Minimum Coverage Requirements

  • 📌 Bodily Injury: $50,000 per person / $100,000 per accident
  • 📌 Property Damage: $50,000 per accident
  • 📌 Uninsured Motorist: $50,000 per person / $100,000 per accident
  • 📌 Uninsured Property Damage: $50,000 per accident

Penalties for No Insurance

  • • $50-$150 fine for first offense
  • • License suspension possible
  • • SR-22 requirement for 3 years
  • • Higher insurance rates

Proof of Insurance

  • • Keep in vehicle at all times
  • • Digital proof accepted on phone
  • • Required for registration
  • • Must show if requested by police

⚠️ Important: While these are the minimum requirements, consider higher limits to better protect your assets. Most accidents exceed minimum coverage limits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common auto insurance questions

What factors affect my auto insurance rates?

Your rates are influenced by your driving record, age, location, vehicle type, credit score, coverage levels, and eligible discounts. Safe drivers with good credit typically enjoy lower rates.

Should I choose a higher deductible?

A higher deductible lowers your premium but means more out-of-pocket costs if you file a claim. Consider your financial situation and driving habits when choosing.

What's the difference between comprehensive and collision?

Collision covers damage from accidents with other vehicles or objects. Comprehensive covers non-collision damage like theft, vandalism, weather, and animal strikes.

Do I need uninsured motorist coverage?

Yes, it's required in North Carolina. This coverage protects you when an uninsured or underinsured driver causes an accident, covering your medical bills and vehicle damage.

How can I lower my insurance premium?

Bundle policies, maintain a clean driving record, improve your credit score, take a defensive driving course, and ask about all available discounts. Shopping around annually also helps.
